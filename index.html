Medical Scientist

What Do They Do

Medical scientists carry out research that further the understanding of human diseases and the improvement of human health. They participate in clinical investigation, research and development, or other related activities.

What They Do On the Job

  1. They adhere to strict safety protocols when handling toxic materials to avoid contamination.
  2. They study the effects of drugs, gases, pesticides, parasites, and microorganisms at a variety of levels.
  3. They plan and lead studies to investigate human or animal disease, preventive methods, and treatments for disease.

Knowledge

Medical scientists should know these subjects:

  • Biology
  • Chemistry
  • Medicine and Dentistry
  • English Language
  • Computers and Electronics

Skills

The skills of a medical scientist include:

  • Writing down things for co-workers and customers
  • Figuring out how to use new ideas and things
  • Noticing a problem and discovering the best solution
  • Listing the pros and cons of various options and choosing the best one
  • Figuring out how a system should operate and how future changes will affect it

Abilities

Medical Scientists should be able to:

  • Communicate by speaking and writing
  • Make general rules or come up with solutions from lots of detailed information
  • Group things in a variety of ways
  • Choose the type of math to answer a problem
  • Do arithmetic
  • Discover hidden patterns

Education

Medical scientists usually need a doctoral degree or post-doctoral training.

Salary

On average, medical scientists earn $99,930. The bottom 10% of people in this career earn $58,190 or less, and the top 10% of people in this career earn $170,260 or more.

Why Pursue This Career?

Someone might like pursuing this career because they love studying human biology. They are fascinated by the anatomy, physiology, and workings of the body. Additionally, they would like to work in the medical field, so they can cure or treat human diseases such as cancer or HIV. Another reason could be they enjoy studying microorganisms such as parasites, bacteria, or viruses and their effect on the human body. They could also enjoy science fairs that involve investigating and researching topics or ideas in science especially in human biology.
